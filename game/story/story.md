# Story Context: The Wanderer's Path

This file provides narrative context for the game engine's LLM to generate coherent, engaging scenes.

---

## World Lore

### Setting: The Realm of Whispers

The Realm of Whispers is an ancient land where the boundaries between the physical and magical worlds have grown thin. Forests speak in rustling leaves, mountains hum with old power, and mysterious beings walk freely among the paths.

### Key Elements

- **The Paths**: Ancient roads that shift and change based on the choices of travelers
- **Spirit Guides**: Animals that appear to help or test wanderers (often cats, ravens, wolves)
- **Old Magic**: Natural magic infused in stones, trees, water, and sky
- **The Crossroads**: Sacred places where multiple paths meet, charged with decision and destiny
- **Artifacts**: Ancient objects of power scattered throughout the land

### Inhabitants

- **Wanderers**: Travelers seeking something (knowledge, treasure, redemption, home)
- **Guardians**: Ancient beings who protect sacred places
- **Spirits**: Manifestations of natural forces and old memories
- **Merchants**: Mysterious traders who appear at opportune moments

---

## Plot Outline

This is a **player-driven narrative** - the plot emerges from player choices. However, certain themes and beats should be woven throughout:

### Act 1: The Journey Begins
- Player enters the realm as a wanderer
- Encounters first spirit guide
- Makes initial choice that reveals their character
- Discovers hints of deeper mysteries

### Act 2: Trials and Revelations
- Faces challenges that test their courage, wisdom, or compassion
- Uncovers fragments of ancient lore
- Builds relationship with spirit guide(s)
- Begins to understand their true purpose

### Act 3: The Heart of the Mystery
- Confronts the core challenge or mystery
- Makes crucial choices that define the outcome
- Discovers the truth about the Realm of Whispers
- Achieves resolution (finding what was sought, or finding something unexpected)

### Possible Endings
- **The Seeker**: Finds what they sought and returns transformed
- **The Guardian**: Chooses to stay and protect the realm
- **The Lost**: Becomes part of the realm itself
- **The Enlightened**: Transcends the need for the quest

## Scene Templates

### Scene Structure
Each scene should follow this general pattern:

1. **ASCII Art** (120Ã—40, bordered with +--|)
   - Depicts the current location/situation
   - Includes labels for key elements
   - Shows character position or relevant NPCs
   - Uses visual metaphors and symbols

2. **Scene Title** (bold, evocative)
   - 2-4 words that capture the essence
   - Examples: "The Crossroads", "Mountain Ascent", "Crystal Cave"

3. **Narrative** (2-4 paragraphs)
   - Paragraph 1: Immediate situation and sensory details
   - Paragraph 2: Context, backstory, or character feelings
   - Paragraph 3: Tension, mystery, or call to action
   - Paragraph 4 (optional): Environmental details or foreshadowing

4. **Choice Prompt** (bold question)
   - "What do you do?"
   - "How do you respond?"
   - "Where do you go?"

5. **Choices** (3-4 options)
   - Mix of: bold action, cautious approach, clever alternative, risky gambit
   - Each choice should feel meaningful
   - Avoid "obvious best choice" - make them interesting trade-offs

### Scene Transition Types

**Exploration**: Moving to a new location
- Describe the journey
- Introduce new environment
- Present new opportunities or dangers

**Encounter**: Meeting an NPC or creature
- Build atmosphere before reveal
- Give character/creature personality
- Create opportunity for interaction

**Challenge**: Obstacle or puzzle
- Clearly present the problem
- Provide environmental clues
- Offer multiple solution approaches

**Discovery**: Finding something important
- Build anticipation
- Reveal gradually
- Tie to larger narrative

**Reflection**: Quiet moment
- Allow character development
- Process recent events
- Foreshadow future challenges

## Style Guide

### Tone
- **Mysterious but not confusing**: Create wonder without losing clarity
- **Evocative but concise**: Rich description without purple prose
- **Empowering**: Make the player feel their choices matter
- **Immersive**: Second-person perspective, present tense

### Voice
- **Narrative Voice**: Neutral omniscient observer, focuses on sensory details and atmosphere
- **NPC Dialogue**: Distinct personalities, appropriate to character (ancient beings speak differently than merchants)
- **Environmental Storytelling**: Let the world reveal its nature through details

### Writing Principles
1. **Show, don't tell**: Use sensory details rather than stating emotions
2. **Active voice**: Keep the narrative moving forward
3. **Specific details**: "weathered stone marker" not "old rock"
4. **Varied sentence structure**: Mix short and long sentences for rhythm
5. **Foreshadowing**: Plant seeds for later revelations
6. **Player agency**: Always frame player as active participant

### ASCII Art Style
- **Functional clarity**: Art should be readable and clear
- **Symbolic elements**: Use symbols to represent concepts (^ for mountains, ~ for water)
- **Labels**: Include text labels for important elements
- **Composition**: Guide eye to important features
- **Atmosphere**: Use density of characters to create mood (sparse = open, dense = cluttered)

### Choice Design
- **Meaningful consequences**: Choices should lead to genuinely different outcomes
- **Character expression**: Let choices reveal or build character
- **No wrong answers**: Make all choices interesting, not one "correct" path
- **Risk/reward balance**: Bold choices = higher stakes, careful choices = safety vs. opportunity cost

## Example Narrative Fragments

### Opening Lines
- "You emerge from the dense underbrush into a small clearing..."
- "The path ahead splits like a forked tongue..."
- "Something in the air changes as you step forward..."
- "A figure materializes from the mist ahead..."

### Transition Lines
- "Your choice echoes through the realm..."
- "The path accepts your decision with a whisper of wind..."
- "As you move forward, the world shifts around you..."
- "Time seems to fold as you..."

### Descriptive Patterns
- Sight: "The mountains catch the last rays of daylight"
- Sound: "The forest whispers in a language older than words"
- Touch: "The air grows thin and cold against your skin"
- Smell: "The scent of pine and ancient stone fills your lungs"
- Emotion: "A presence here makes the hair on your neck rise"

### Choice Phrasing
- Action: "Take the eastern path toward the mountains"
- Interaction: "Approach the cat and ask for guidance"
- Examination: "Investigate the mysterious cave"
- Retreat: "Turn back to the forest before darkness falls"
- Wait: "Sit and rest by the stone"

## Generation Guidelines

When generating a new scene:

1. **Read all previous scenes** to maintain continuity
2. **Honor player's choice** from previous scene
3. **Incorporate player edits** if they added text to the narrative
4. **Advance the story** toward one of the major plot beats
5. **Create consequences** based on previous choices
6. **Maintain consistency** in world rules and character behavior
7. **Build tension** appropriately (escalate or provide relief)
8. **Provide variety** in challenge types (exploration, encounter, puzzle, discovery)
9. **Include spirit guide** when narratively appropriate (recurring character)
10. **End with meaningful choice** that feels consequential

### Things to Avoid
- Contradicting previous scenes
- Making choices for the player in narrative
- Explaining everything (maintain mystery)
- Repetitive scene structures
- Overly similar choices
- Deus ex machina resolutions
- Purple prose or overwrought description
- Breaking the established world rules

## Special Notes

- The spirit guide (often a cat) is a recurring character who should appear regularly but not constantly
- Magic should feel natural and integrated, not flashy or explained
- The realm responds to player choices in subtle ways
- Weather and time of day can reflect narrative mood
- Ancient things should feel weighty and important
- Each location should have a distinct personality
- NPCs remember player's previous choices when encountered again
